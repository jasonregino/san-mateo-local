Hillsdale

Hillsdale grew up around the mall. When Hillsdale Shopping Center opened in 1954 on old dairy-farm land, it helped turn this stretch of San Mateo into the busy shopping and dining hub locals still circle through today.

A little history

The Hillsdale you know started with a shopping center. Developer David D. Bohannon assembled hundreds of acres here in the old Beresford district, later renamed Hillsdale, and in November 1954 opened Hillsdale Shopping Center on what had been a dairy farm. It was an open-air plaza of wide walkways and palm-lined paths built for the postwar car, anchored by a brand-new Macy's that opened its doors that November.

For a generation of locals, the heart of Hillsdale was not a department store, it was the Farmer's Market at the north end, past 31st Avenue. This open-air hall had a grocery, a produce section, a butcher, and one of the Peninsula's first international food courts, with spots people still name from memory: the Capri Room, a cocktail restaurant tucked in a corner, alongside the Hofbrau, the Steak Inn, and Chinese food. You would sit at round tables inlaid with the market's rooster logo, in blue vinyl bucket chairs, under round skylights that poured in the light. Plenty of people came as much to socialize as to shop, and more than one still remembers a grandmother who was there five days a week.

Over the decades the big anchors came and went. Sears, The Emporium, J.J. Newberry, and Mervyn's joined Macy's, and today Nordstrom holds down a corner. Locals still bring up the little things, like the wishing fountain near the stairs where you would toss in a coin, until it was removed in 1977 during the drought. The open-air center was enclosed into a two-level mall in 1981, and it has kept reinventing itself ever since, pivoting toward an experiential mix of dining, a movie theater, and shops as the newer Bay Meadows blocks filled in right next door.

Famous facts

  • Hillsdale Shopping Center opened in November 1954 on former dairy-farm land.
  • It was developed by David D. Bohannon, who assembled the land in the old Beresford district, later renamed Hillsdale.
  • The center was anchored by a brand-new Macy's that opened on November 19, 1954.
  • The beloved open-air Farmer's Market had 22 departments and eateries locals still name: the Capri Room, the Hofbrau, and the Steak Inn.
  • A wishing fountain near the stairs was a fixture until it was removed in 1977 during the drought.
  • Anchor stores over the years included Sears, The Emporium, J.J. Newberry, and Mervyn's, with Macy's and Nordstrom there today.
  • The open-air plaza was enclosed into a two-level mall in 1981.
