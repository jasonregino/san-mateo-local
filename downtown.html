Downtown San Mateo

Downtown is where San Mateo began. Laid out alongside the railroad in 1863, the blocks around B Street and Central Park are still the city's beating heart, home to the restaurants, the bars, the farmers' market, and the Caltrain that started it all.

A little history

San Mateo's downtown owes its existence to the railroad. In 1863, Charles B. Polhemus, a director of the new San Francisco to San Jose line, laid out the town's core beside the tracks, and on October 17 of that year the first train made the run from San Francisco to San Mateo in just thirty-seven minutes. The station came first, and buildings quickly followed around Main Street and Railroad Avenue, and downtown was born. Nearby, the land that is now Central Park had been part of Polhemus's own estate, purchased back in 1854 and later landscaped, in the 1880s, by John McLaren, the same man who would go on to shape Golden Gate Park. More than a hundred and sixty years later, downtown is still organized around those first rail blocks, now filled with the independent spots that make San Mateo feel like San Mateo.

Famous facts

  • Downtown was laid out in 1863 alongside the new San Francisco to San Jose railroad.
  • The first train reached San Mateo on October 17, 1863, making the trip from San Francisco in thirty-seven minutes.
  • Central Park was once part of Charles B. Polhemus's private estate, purchased in 1854.
  • Central Park's grounds were landscaped in the 1880s by John McLaren, who later designed Golden Gate Park.
  • The B Street corridor is still the city's main dining and nightlife strip.

Things to Do

Central Park & Japanese Garden

park · Free

A sixteen-acre downtown park with a 1960s Japanese tea garden and koi ponds, a kids playground, and tennis courts. The beloved miniature train that ran here since the late 1940s is retired for now while the city looks for a replacement, but the garden and playground keep it a downtown favorite.

Kiddo Adventure Indoor Playground

family · $$

A downtown indoor playground for kids up to about ten, with slides, a zipline, and obstacle courses.

San Mateo Public Library (Main)

attraction · Free

The city's main library branch with a big children's area, story times, and free community programs near downtown.

Central Park Music Series

event · Free

Free outdoor concerts in Central Park on summer evenings, a San Mateo tradition of blankets, picnics, and live bands under the trees.

Cinemark Century San Mateo 12

entertainment · $$

A twelve-screen downtown movie theater within walking distance of B Street's restaurants and parking garages.

Color Me Mine San Mateo

classes · $$

A walk-in paint-your-own-pottery studio where families pick bisque pieces and glaze them for kiln firing.

Art Academia

classes · $$

A European-style art school teaching academic drawing, painting, and ceramics to kids and adults.
