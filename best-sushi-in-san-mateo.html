The Best Sushi in San Mateo

Updated Updated July 2, 2026

From a Michelin-starred omakase counter to the neighborhood roll spot, San Mateo's Japanese food runs deep.

San Mateo has quietly been one of the Bay Area's best sushi towns for years. Here's the range, from a bucket-list omakase to the everyday roll spot, plus the legend that lives on under a new name.

The omakase

Sushi Yoshizumi

Michelin-starred omakase · Downtown

A tiny eight-seat cypress counter serving Edomae-style omakase. Chef Akira Yoshizumi earned a Michelin star here, and the room feels like a hushed sushi bar in Japan. The bucket-list splurge.

Sushi Edomata

Edomae omakase · 25th Ave

The continuation of the beloved Sushi Sam's, run by Sam's nephew and family. Reservations-only Edomae omakase at a lively bar that still draws the old regulars.

Sushi Maruyama

Omakase · Downtown

An intimate room with fish flown in from Tsukiji, a deep omakase and nigiri list, and A5 wagyu. High quality without the tasting-menu sticker shock.

The everyday spots

Sapporo Rock-N-Roll Sushi

Aburi + rolls · South El Camino

Opened in 2021 by two childhood friends. Torch-seared aburi nigiri, Japanese-influenced tapas, signature rolls, and a fun early happy hour.

Joy Sushi

Neighborhood sushi · Downtown (B St)

A long-running downtown favorite with a big menu of rolls, nigiri, and combos. The reliable, unpretentious neighborhood bar people have gone to for years.

Izakaya Mai

Izakaya · Downtown

A longstanding downtown izakaya with nigiri and rolls plus grilled meats, fried tapas, and curry bowls. The group spot when not everyone wants raw fish.

Kazu Sushi & Sando

Takeout omakase boxes · 21st Ave

A family-run takeout-only spot doing omakase sushi boxes plus chicken katsu and fruit sandos, ready in about fifteen minutes. Best value grab-and-go.

One honest note: if you want conveyor-belt sushi, you'll have to leave town, there isn't a kaiten spot inside San Mateo right now. Know a sushi bar we missed? Tell us at hello@sanmateolocal.com.
