Things To Do in San Mateo

Parks, trails, the bayfront, and ways to spend a day. The outdoor San Mateo a local actually uses.

★ Local's pick Trail

San Francisco Bay Trail (Coyote Point to the Bridge)

Bayfront · Free

The San Mateo stretch of the Bay Trail runs the shoreline from Coyote Point south to the San Mateo Bridge and on toward Foster City, with jets dropping into SFO across the water, sailboats and kiteboarders on the bay, and wide-open views the whole way. Walk it, run it, bike it, or fish along it.

★ Local's pick Park

Seal Point Park

Bayfront · Free

The old San Mateo landfill, reborn as a grassy hill with 360-degree bay views, public art, a 3-acre dog park, and a windsurf and kite launch. Climb the mound near sunset for the best free view in town.

DirectionsWebsite
Park

Coyote Point Recreation Area

Bayfront · Parking fee

A 670-acre bayfront county park with a eucalyptus-shaded shoreline, a beach and marina, picnic groves, the CuriOdyssey science and wildlife museum, and the steady summer winds that make it a top Bay Area kiteboarding spot.
