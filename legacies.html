San Mateo Legacies

The diners, the shops, the family-run spots that have been part of San Mateo since the 1980s and before, and are still open today. The ones that outlasted every trend. Walk in and say hi.

Nominate a legacy spot
Est.1905

Wisnom's / Hassett Ace Hardware

Hardware · 545 1st Ave

The historic Wisnom's hardware site has anchored downtown since 1905, back when it was the family lumberyard. It's a hardware store to this day, keeping San Mateo's century-old fix-it-yourself tradition alive.

Est.1906

Takahashi Market

Market & deli · 221 S Claremont St

Four generations deep, founded by Issei immigrant Tokutaro Takahashi in 1906. The surviving anchor of San Mateo's lost Japantown, still slinging Japanese and Hawaiian groceries and plate lunches.

Est.1930

Victor Shoe Repair

Cobbler · 240 S B St

A downtown mom-and-pop cobbler since 1930, mending shoes, leather, and luggage the way they used to. Generations of San Mateans have brought their soles here.

Est.1933

Ah Sam Floral Co.

Florist · 2645 S El Camino Real

A third-generation family florist founded in 1933, a Peninsula go-to for weddings, funerals, and everyday bouquets for nearly a century.

Est.1937

Steiner's Jewelry

Jeweler · 231 S San Mateo Dr

Ted Steiner opened these doors in 1937, and three generations later it's still the trusted downtown name for fine jewelry and watch repair.

Est.1948

Ravioli House

Italian deli · 505 S B St

Handmade ravioli and Italian deli sandwiches from the same family operation since 1948. A downtown lunch institution where the pasta is still made in house.

Est.1955

The Swingin' Door

English pub · 106 E 25th Ave

This spot has poured pints as a San Mateo bar since 1955. It isn't a family dynasty, it's independently run today, but few places have been a neighborhood gathering spot for this long, which is exactly why it belongs here.

Est.1958

Bundy Opticians

Optician · 201 S San Mateo Dr

Andrew Bundy opened his optical shop in 1958, and for decades the Bundy family hand-fit eyewear for the whole town. It recently passed to new hands, but the name and the craft carry on.

Est.1967

Antone's Cocktail Lounge

Neighborhood bar · 32 37th Ave

A family-run dive since 1967, with pool, darts, and a jukebox. The kind of unpretentious local watering hole that gets harder to find every year.

Est.1968

Romolo's Cannoli

Sicilian bakery · 81 W 37th Ave

Sicilian cannoli and spumoni made to order since 1968, now run by the founders' grandson. Proof that some recipes are worth keeping in the family.

Est.1972

Bob's Barber Stylist

Barber · 114 W 25th Ave

A family barbershop in the same 25th Avenue chair since 1972, cutting hair for men, women, and kids across generations.

Est.1984

Luceti's on 25th

Italian · 109 W 25th Ave

A family-owned Italian restaurant on 25th Avenue serving the classics for four decades.

Est.1987

Porterhouse

Steakhouse · 164 S B St

Bruno Ugur has run this corner since he bought the old Bogies in 1987. It's been reborn as an in-house dry-aging steakhouse, but the same family's been here the whole time.

Est.1990

The Pantry

Diner · 1855 S Delaware St

A family-run diner blending classic American breakfast with Filipino home cooking since 1990, walls covered in family photos and decades of regulars.

Honorable Mentions

A little newer than the rest, but beloved all the same.

La Piñata

· Est. 1994

Independent and adored since 1994, a hair too new for the main list but a San Mateo favorite through and through.
